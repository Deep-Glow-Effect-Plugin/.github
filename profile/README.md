## 🚀 Introduction
Deep Glow for Windows is a glow effect that runs inside Adobe After Effects, built to make luminous, realistic bloom easy to achieve. Where a hand-built glow often means juggling blurs, exposure tricks, and blend modes, Deep Glow condenses the whole look into one effect you can apply and shape in seconds. Its energy-conserving model spreads light the way it behaves in the real world, so titles, logos, and video gain a warm, glowing quality while their highlights stay clean and controlled. Across the current generation of the plugin, the emphasis stays on speed, quality, and results you can repeat.

## ⭐ Highlights

### Look and Quality
- **Energy-conserving bloom** — Bright pixels spill light naturally, giving a believable, cinematic glow instead of a flat white wash.
- **Consistent results** — The After Effects deep glow effect reacts predictably from shot to shot, so a look holds together across a project.
- **Preset library** — Ready-made starting points let you land a polished glow quickly and refine from there.

### Performance and Control
- **GPU acceleration** — Rendering runs on your graphics hardware for smooth previews and fast output.
- **Focused controls** — Radius, exposure, threshold, and color settings keep tuning intuitive rather than overwhelming.
- **Layer-friendly** — Apply it to text, graphics, or footage and the glow travels with the layer through your comp.

## 💻 System Specs

**Minimum**
- OS: Windows 10 64-bit
- Host: A supported version of Adobe After Effects
- Processor: A modern 64-bit multi-core processor
- Memory: 8 GB RAM
- Graphics: A GPU that meets After Effects' minimum requirements
- Storage: Room for the plugin and your project files

**Recommended**
- OS: Windows 11 64-bit
- Host: A recent version of Adobe After Effects
- Processor: A high-performance multi-core processor
- Memory: 16 GB RAM or more
- Graphics: A dedicated GPU with generous video memory

## 🎯 Getting Up and Running
1. Open your project and pick the layer you want to illuminate.
2. Apply Deep Glow from the Effects & Presets panel.
3. Load a preset to start quickly, or set the radius and exposure yourself.
4. Adjust color and threshold until the bloom feels right for the scene.
5. Preview and render — the glow renders as part of your composition.

## ❓ Common Questions
**Is Deep Glow free?**
Deep Glow is a paid plugin. A demo or trial may be available so you can evaluate the effect first, but the full plugin is a commercial product rather than a free full copy.

**Which versions of Windows are supported?**
Deep Glow runs as an After Effects plugin on 64-bit Windows 10 and Windows 11. Provided your Adobe After Effects install is supported on your Windows version, Deep Glow will run with it.

**Is my data secure?**
Deep Glow processes footage locally as an effect inside After Effects on your own system, so your media and project files stay on your machine.
